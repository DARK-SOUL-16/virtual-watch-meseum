Founded: 1839

Founder: Antoni Patek and Franciszek Czapek

History of Patek Philippe

Patek Philippewas founded in 1839 by Antoni Patek and Franciszek Czapek. The Swiss brand is renowned for its high-quality craftsmanship and tradition of creating some of the world's most expensive and intricate timepieces. Patek Philippe revolutionized watchmaking by introducing innovations such as the perpetual calendar, split-seconds chronograph, and minute repeater.

10 Notable Patek Philippe


1.Patek Philippe Calatrava

Material: 18k gold, stainless steel

Made: 1932

Price: Approx. $20,000 - $50,000

Why It’s Popular: Known for its simple and elegant design, the **Calatrava** is considered one of the most iconic luxury dress watches. Its timeless aesthetic and precision have made it highly sought after by collectors.


2. Patek Philippe Nautilus

Material: Stainless steel, 18k white gold, diamonds

Made: 1976

Price: Approx. $30,000 - $200,000

Why It’s Popular: The **Nautilus** combines a luxury sports watch design with robust features, making it one of the most desirable pieces in the luxury watch world. Its distinctive octagonal bezel and unique design make it a standout.


3. Patek Philippe Grand Complications

Material: 18k gold, platinum

Made: 1989

Price: Approx. $200,000+

Why It’s Popular: The **Grand Complications** collection includes watches with intricate features like minute repeaters, perpetual calendars, and chronographs. These models highlight Patek Philippe's mastery of complications in watchmaking.


4. Patek Philippe Aquanaut

Material: Stainless steel, rubber strap

Made: 1997

Price: Approx. $30,000 - $50,000

Why It’s Popular: The **Aquanaut** was designed as a sportier, more modern alternative to the Nautilus, featuring a more casual design with a unique rubber strap. It's one of the most sought-after sport watches in luxury horology.


5. Patek Philippe Perpetual Calendar Chronograph

Material: Stainless steel, 18k yellow gold

Made: 1941

Price: Approx. $200,000+

Why It’s Popular: The **Perpetual Calendar Chronograph** is a masterpiece of technical watchmaking, combining both a perpetual calendar and a chronograph in one. It's regarded as a major achievement in horology.


6. Patek Philippe 5207

Material: Platinum, 18k gold

Made: 2008

Price: Approx. $600,000+

Why It’s Popular: The **5207** is a striking example of Patek Philippe's technical expertise, featuring a minute repeater, tourbillon, and perpetual calendar. It's an incredible example of Patek's dedication to fine craftsmanship.


7. Patek Philippe Calatrava Pilot Travel Time

Material: 18k white gold, leather strap

Made: 2015

Price: Approx. $45,000 - $60,000

Why It’s Popular: A more recent model, the Calatrava Pilot Travel Time** combines aviation-inspired design with the timeless elegance of the Calatrava line, and it features dual-time-zone functionality.


8. Patek Philippe 5110 World Time

Material: Yellow gold case, enamel dial

Made: 2000

Price: Around $35,000+

Why It’s Popular: The 5110 features Patek Philippe's iconic World Time complication, which displays the time in 24 time zones simultaneously. The watch is both a technical marvel and a beautifully crafted piece, with a stunning enamel dial.


9.Patek Philippe 1518

Material: Stainless steel case

Made: 1941

Price: $11 million+ (auction price)

Why It’s Popular: The Patek Philippe 1518 is famous for being the first perpetual calendar chronograph ever produced in series. It’s regarded as one of the most important watches in the history of horology due to its complexity and the fact that it was produced in very limited numbers.


10.Patek Philippe Sky Moon Tourbillon

Material: Platinum, 18k gold

Made: 2001

Price: Approx. $1 million+

Why It’s Popular: The **Sky Moon Tourbillon** is one of Patek Philippe's most complicated watches, featuring 12 complications, including a minute repeater and a tourbillon. It's an example of the pinnacle of watchmaking, blending art and mechanics.
