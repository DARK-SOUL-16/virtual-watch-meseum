Seiko Watches

Founded:1881, Tokyo, Japan (originally as Hattori Tokei-ten, later became Seiko)

Founder: Kintaro Hattori

History of Seiko

Seiko is one of the most influential and innovative watch brands in the world, known for its pioneering technology and high-quality craftsmanship. Below is an overview of Seiko's history, notable watches, and what makes them popular.Seiko is known for producing high-quality watches at various price points, ranging from affordable timepieces to luxury offerings like Grand Seiko. The brand is also recognized for its work in sports and diving watches, as well as its contribution to the automatic movement industry.

First Watch of Seiko

Seiko Laurel

Material: Titanium and ceramic

Made: 1960s

Price: $100 to $1,000


10 Notable watches of Seiko

1. Seiko Astron

Material:Stainless steel

Made: 1969

Price: $500 to $3,000+

Why It’s Popular:The Seiko Astron was the world’s first quartz watch, revolutionizing the watch industry. It is iconic for its precision and technological breakthrough, which paved the way for the widespread use of quartz movements.

2.Seiko 5 Sports

Material: Stainless steel

Made:1963

Price: $50 to $300+

Why It’s Popular: The Seiko 5 Sports is one of Seiko's most beloved models due to its durability, reliability, and affordable price. With a design that includes a day-date complication and automatic movement, it has been a popular choice for watch enthusiasts for decades.

3.Seiko Diver’s 200m

Material: Stainless steel

Made:1965

Price:$200 to $500+

Why It’s Popular: Seiko's dive watches, like the Seiko Diver's 200m, are known for their rugged design and extreme reliability. This model became a staple for both professional divers and casual watch enthusiasts due to its water resistance and robustness.

4.Seiko Presage Cocktail Time

Material: Stainless steel, enamel dial

Made: 2010

Price: $300 to $600+

Why It’s Popular: The Presage Cocktail Time series is famous for its stunning sunburst dial, which features a unique design inspired by cocktails. This collection is known for offering luxury-level aesthetics at an affordable price point, making it a popular choice among watch collectors.

5.Seiko Grand Seiko Spring Drive

Material: Stainless steel, platinum, gold

Made: 1999

Price: $5,000 to $30,000+

Why It’s Popular: The Spring Drive is a unique Seiko movement that combines mechanical and quartz technologies, offering unmatched accuracy and a smooth sweep of the second hand. It has become a symbol of Seiko’s commitment to innovation and craftsmanship, making it highly sought after.

6.Seiko Turtle

Material: Stainless steel, silicone strap

Made:1976 (reissued in 2016)

Price: $300 to $500+

Why It’s Popular: The Seiko Turtle dive watch is named for its distinctive cushion-shaped case. Known for its robust design, automatic movement, and legendary water resistance, it’s a fan favorite in the diving watch community.

7.Seiko 6139 Pogue

Material: Stainless steel, tachymeter beze

Made: 1970s

Price: $500 to $1,500+

Why It’s Popular:The Seiko 6139 is the first automatic chronograph, making it a historic piece. This model became famous for being worn by NASA astronaut William Pogue during the Skylab mission. Its unique design and historical significance make it a collector's item.

8. Seiko Monster

Material: Stainless steel, rubber strap

Made: Early 2000s

Price: $200 to $400+

Why It’s Popular:The Seiko Monster dive watch features an aggressive design with a highly legible dial and is known for its ruggedness and affordability. Its bold aesthetic and high performance make it a popular choice among diving enthusiasts.

9.Seiko Solar

Material: Stainless steel, solar-powered movement

Made: 2000s

Price: $100 to $400

Why It’s Popular:The Seiko Solar collection uses solar energy to power the watch, offering convenience and eco-friendliness. Its practical features and affordable price have made it a hit among environmentally-conscious buyers.

10.Seiko Grand Seiko SBGJ237

Material: Stainless steel, 18K gold accents

Made: 2017

Price: $7,000 to $15,000+

Why It’s Popular: The SBGJ237 is a part of the Grand Seiko collection and features a high-end Spring Drive movement, offering precision and craftsmanship at the highest level. Known for its exquisite dial and mechanical excellence, it represents Seiko's luxury watchmaking prowess.
