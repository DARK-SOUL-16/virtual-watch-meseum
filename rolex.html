Rolex Watches

Founded: 1905, London, UK (originally as "Wilsdorf & Davis") renamed to Rolex in 1908

Founder: Hans Wilsdorf and Alfred Davis

History of Rolex

Rolex was founded by Hans Wilsdorf and his brother-in-law Alfred Davis in London in 1905. Initially, the company was called Wilsdorf & Davis, but they later changed the name to Rolex in 1908. Hans Wilsdorf was determined to create the world's most precise and durable wristwatch, and by 1910, Rolex became the first watch brand to receive a Swiss Certificate of Chronometric Precision.

10 Notable Rolex Watches


1.Rolex Oyster Perpetual

Material:Stainless steel

Made: 1931 (with the introduction of the Perpetual movement)

Price: $5,000 to $10,000+

Why It’s Popular: The Oyster Perpetual is the foundation of Rolex's iconic design, combining both the waterproof Oyster case and the self-winding Perpetual movement. It is celebrated for its precision, simplicity, and durability, representing the essence of Rolex.

2.Rolex Submariner

Material: Stainless steel, 18K gold, ceramic

Made:1953

Price: $8,000 to $40,000+ (depending on the model and materials)

Why It’s Popular: The Submariner is one of the most iconic dive watches in the world, known for its water resistance (up to 300 meters) and durability. It is favored by divers, collectors, and enthusiasts for its rugged performance and timeless design.

3.Rolex Datejust

Material: Stainless steel, 18K gold, platinum, diamonds

Made:1945

Price: $7,000 to $30,000+

Why It’s Popular: The Datejust is the quintessential dress watch, first introduced in 1945 as the first wristwatch to display the date on the dial. Known for its elegance and versatility, it has remained a symbol of success for decades.


4.Rolex Day-Date (President)

Material: 18K yellow gold, platinum, diamonds

Made: 1956

Price:$12,000 to $50,000+ (varies based on model and materials)

Why It’s Popular: The Day-Date is often referred to as the “President” watch due to its association with high-profile leaders, including U.S. presidents. It is one of the most prestigious watches in the world, known for its day and date display and its luxurious design.


5.Rolex GMT-Master II

Material: Stainless steel, 18K gold, ceramic

Made: 1955

Price: $9,000 to $30,000+

Why It’s Popular: The GMT-Master II was originally designed for pilots to track two time zones simultaneously. Known for its rotatable bezel and dual time zone functionality, it has become a favorite among travelers and collectors.


6.Rolex Sea-Dweller

Material: Stainless steel, 18K gold, ceramic

Made:1967

Price: $10,000 to $15,000+

Why It’s Popular: Designed for professional divers, the Sea-Dweller is known for its extreme water resistance (up to 4,000 feet on the newer models) and helium release valve. It’s a favorite for those needing a durable watch that can withstand extreme underwater conditions.


7.Rolex Daytona

Material: Stainless steel, 18K gold, platinum

Made: 1963

Price: $15,000 to $100,000+

Why It’s Popular:The Daytona is Rolex’s chronograph model, designed for motorsports enthusiasts. It is known for its precision timing, stylish design, and association with the legendary actor and race car driver Paul Newman, whose model became one of the most expensive watches ever sold.


8. Rolex Explorer

Material: Stainless steel, 18K white gold

Made:1953

Price: $6,000 to $15,000+

Why It’s Popular:The Explorer was made for adventurers and explorers. It is best known for its association with Sir Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay during the first successful summit of Mount Everest in 1953. The Explorer remains popular for its simplicity, legibility, and connection to exploration.


9. Rolex Milgauss

Material: Stainless steel, 18K white gold

Made: 1956

Price: $8,000 to $10,000+

Why It’s Popular:The Milgauss is designed for scientists and professionals working in magnetic fields. It is known for its anti-magnetic properties and its unique lightning bolt second hand. Its distinctive look and technical features make it a unique and attractive option for Rolex enthusiasts.


10.Rolex Yacht-Master

Material: Stainless steel, 18K yellow gold, platinum, Everose gold

Made: 1992

Price: $9,000 to $40,000+

Why It’s Popular: The Yacht-Master is designed for those with a passion for sailing and the sea. It combines luxury and sportiness, featuring a rotatable bezel and a nautical-inspired design. It’s a favorite among those seeking a versatile yet luxurious watch for both formal and casual occasions.
