History of Cartier

Cartier is a French luxury goods brand that is widely recognized for its exquisite watches, jewelry, and accessories. The brand was founded in 1847 by Louis-François Cartier, who took over a small jewelry workshop in Paris. Over the years, Cartier became known for its innovative and high-quality watchmaking, as well as its iconic designs. The company’s reputation for craftsmanship and luxury was cemented when it began designing watches for royal families and celebrities. Cartier's watches have been known for their elegance, precision, and craftsmanship, blending classic designs with cutting-edge watchmaking technology. Over time, Cartier became a leader in the luxury watch industry, producing some of the most iconic and recognizable timepieces in the world, such as the Santos, the Tank, and the Ballon Bleu.

10 Notable Cartier Watches

1.Cartier Tank

Material: 18k yellow gold, steel

Made: 1917

Price:Approx. $4,000 - $30,000+ (depending on model)

Why It’s Popular: The Tank is one of the most iconic watches in Cartier’s collection, known for its rectangular design inspired by military tank tracks. Over the years, it has been worn by countless celebrities and royalty, including Jacqueline Kennedy and Princess Diana, symbolizing timeless elegance.


2.Ballon Bleu de Cartier

Material: Stainless steel, 18k gold, diamonds

Made: 2007

Price:Approx. $5,000 - $35,000 (depending on material and customization)

Why It’s Popular:The Ballon Bleu features a rounded case with a signature blue sapphire cabochon on the winding crown. Its modern, refined design combined with a bold aesthetic makes it a highly sought-after model for both men and women.


3.Cartier Pasha

Material: Stainless steel, 18k gold, diamonds

Made: 1985

Price:$4,000 - $50,000 (depending on material and configuration)

Why It’s Popular:Originally designed for the Sultan of Morocco, the Pasha is known for its distinctive screw-down crown cover. It has a bold, sporty design that remains popular due to its connection to royalty and its status as a statement piece.


4.Ronde de Cartier

Material: Stainless steel, 18k gold, diamonds

Made: 2000

Price:$4,000- $12,000 (varies by model and materials)

Why It’s Popular: The Ronde de Cartier is a classic round watch with a timeless design, featuring large Roman numerals on its dial. Its simplicity and elegance have made it a staple for those seeking a luxury watch that’s both understated and sophisticated.


5.Cartier Calibre de Cartier

Material: Stainless steel, 18k gold, rubber, leather

Made: 2010

Price: $5,000 - $15,000 (depending on material and customization)

Why It’s Popular: The Calibre de Cartier is a larger, more masculine sports watch. It features Cartier’s in-house movement, giving it a modern edge while still retaining the brand’s high standards of craftsmanship and design. Its bold and rugged look appeals to those who want a more contemporary Cartier timepiece.


6.Cartier Drive de Cartier

Material: Stainless steel, 18k gold, leather strap

Made: 2016

Price: $4,000 - $12,000 (depending on material and customization)

Why It’s Popular: The Drive de Cartier features a cushion-shaped case and a refined, minimalist design. It has a distinctive, elegant look that combines classic Cartier lines with a modern twist, making it a versatile and stylish choice for dressy occasions


7.Cartier Crash

Material: 18k gold, stainless steel

Made: 1967

Price: $25,000 - $60,000+ (depending on model and condition)

Why It’s Popular: The Crash is one of Cartier’s most avant-garde and unconventional designs. Its unique, asymmetrical case, resembling a melting watch, was inspired by a car crash. It has become a symbol of artistic expression and is beloved for its quirky, eccentric appeal.


8.Cartier Tortue

Material: Stainless Steel, 18K Yellow Gold, 18K Rose Gold, Leather Strap

Made: 1912 (continuously produced)

Price: $6,000 to $25,000+ (depending on material and customization)

Why It’s Popular: The Tortue is a distinctive, asymmetrical watch with a case that resembles a tortoise shell. Known for its avant-garde design, it is one of Cartier’s more artistic models and is favored by those who want something unique and refined.


9.Cartier Rotonde de Cartier

Material: 18k gold, stainless steel, leather strap

Made: 2002

Price: $10,000 - $30,000 (varies depending on model)

Why It’s Popular: The Rotonde de Cartier features a round case with unique dials and complications, making it an appealing choice for those looking for a distinctive and luxurious timepiece. Its blend of traditional design and intricate mechanics has made it a favorite among collectors.


10.Cartier Tank Americaine

Material: 18k gold, leather

Made: 1989

Price: $7,000 - $20,000 (depending on material)

Why It’s Popular: The Tank Americaine is a sleek and elongated version of the classic Tank design. It has a more modern, elongated case that appeals to those looking for a larger, more contemporary version of the original Tank. Its elegant profile continues to make it a symbol of understated luxury.
